The Parallel Universe Where I Met You

Out of infinite timelines, this is the one we chose.

In another universe, I never replied.

Your words felt sharp.

Guarded. Defensive.

I mistook strength for distance.

And we faded before we began.

But in this universe, I stayed.

We kept talking.

Midnight conversations stretched longer.

The distance between cities started shrinking.

You hesitated when I asked you to come to Bangalore.

But you came.

In some timelines, I never made dosa.

In this one, I tried.

It wasn’t perfect.

You were skeptical.

You tasted it anyway.

Something shifted.

Thirty days together.

Shivanasamudra Falls roaring beside us.

Isha Foundation in quiet stillness.

Nandi Hills at dawn.

Wayanad wrapped in mist.

In another universe, it was casual.

In this one, it wasn’t.

You went back home.

Distance returned.

But thinner.

Marriage wasn’t a fantasy.

It became inevitable.

Convincing families wasn’t easy.

But love outlasted hesitation.

Months later, I flew to Delhi.

Pre-wedding lights. Cameras flashing.

Seeing you again felt unreal.

Like timelines had merged correctly.

Engagement in Aligarh.

You in gold.

I forgot how to breathe.

In other universes, I watched someone else stand beside you.

Not this one.

March 5th, 2024. Haldi.

Laughter spilling everywhere.

March 6th.

You entered in red.

Radiant.

Walking toward me.

In that moment, all other universes felt distant.

Garlands exchanged.

I felt chosen.

Back in Bangalore.

Then the call.

Scientist at Indian Space Research Organisation.

In another universe, doubt won.

In this one, belief did.

Sakleshpur. Udupi.

Four days of relentless rain.

A thousand kilometers on a bike.

Completely soaked.

Completely alive.

Some love stories are written in candlelight.

Ours was written in monsoon rain.

Ahmedabad became home.

Dwarka. Somnath. Nashik.

Daman. Mumbai.

Each city another confirmation.

After Mumbai came the quiet miracle.

You were pregnant.

No fireworks.

Just stillness.

In countless universes, this moment doesn’t exist.

In this one, it does.

There are infinite versions of us.

Ones who never met.

Ones who gave up.

Ones who chose differently.

But this is the universe where I met you.

And I would choose it again. Every time.
